The Foundation

MACRO
BREAKDOWN

Your body uses three macronutrients for energy and recovery. Getting the ratio right is the difference between stalling and thriving.

🥩
PROTEIN
30–40%

The building block of muscle. Aim for 0.7–1g per lb of bodyweight daily. Prioritize lean meats, eggs, dairy, and legumes.

🍚
CARBS
40–50%

Your primary fuel for intense training. Time carb intake around workouts for maximum performance and glycogen replenishment.

🥑
FATS
20–30%

Essential for hormone production and vitamin absorption. Focus on unsaturated sources and omega-3 rich foods.

Optional Supplements

SMART
SUPPLEMENTATION

Supplements don't replace food — they fill gaps. These are the only ones backed by strong evidence:

Creatine Monohydrate

The most researched performance supplement in history. 3–5g daily increases ATP regeneration, strength, and muscle volume. No loading phase needed.

Whey Protein

A convenient way to hit daily protein targets. 25–50g post-workout. Choose isolate for lactose sensitivity. Food-first approach always preferred.

Vitamin D3 + K2

Most people are deficient, especially in low-sunlight climates. Supports testosterone levels, bone density, and immune function. 2,000–4,000 IU D3 daily.

Omega-3 Fish Oil

Reduces inflammation from training, supports joint health, and improves cardiovascular markers. 2–3g EPA+DHA daily from a quality source.

Caffeine (Pre-Workout)

Enhances strength, endurance, and focus. 3–6mg per kg bodyweight 30–45 minutes before training. Black coffee works perfectly — no expensive blends required.

Magnesium Glycinate

Involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions. Improves sleep quality, recovery, and muscle relaxation. Take 300–400mg before bed for best results.

Nutrition FAQ

COMMON
QUESTIONS

Do I need to count calories to see results?

Tracking calories for at least 4–6 weeks builds strong nutritional awareness. After that, many people can maintain results through portion awareness and food quality alone.

How much protein do I really need per day?

Aim for 0.7–1g per pound of bodyweight. For a 180 lb person that's 126–180g. Spread it across 4–6 meals for optimal muscle protein synthesis throughout the day.

Should I eat before or after a workout?

Both matter. Have a balanced meal 60–90 minutes before training. Within 30–60 minutes after, consume fast-digesting protein and carbohydrates to jumpstart recovery.

Are cheat meals okay on this plan?

One planned refeed meal per week is actually beneficial — it restores leptin levels and supports mental sustainability. Avoid turning one cheat meal into a full cheat weekend.
